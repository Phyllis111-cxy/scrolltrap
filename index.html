Reflection · FeedLoop

You said you came for one thing. Your cart tells a different story.

The algorithm didn't push ads at you. It did something subtler — it watched what you paused on, what you liked, what you almost scrolled past but didn't.

Then it quietly rebuilt your feed around those signals. You didn't decide to want these things. You were shown them — repeatedly, at the right moment, in the right format — until wanting felt natural.

That's not a glitch. That's the design.

Question 01

Look at your cart. Which item surprised you the most — and do you actually want it, or does it just feel familiar now?

Think about the difference between genuinely wanting something and having been shown it enough times that it started to feel like your idea.

Question 02

At what point did you notice the feed shifting? Or did you not notice at all — and what does that tell you?

The algorithm works best when it's invisible. If you didn't notice, that's the point.

Question 03

If you opened your real For You page right now, what would you look for differently? Name one specific signal you'd watch for.

Be concrete — a word, a pattern, a feeling. Vague awareness doesn't change behavior.
